What Is a QR Code?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of two-dimensional barcode that can store information such as a URL, text, or contact details.

When scanned with a phone camera or QR scanner, the code instantly opens the stored link or content.

QR codes are widely used for:

  • Websites and landing pages
  • Menus and booking systems
  • Payments and forms
  • Product packaging
  • Events and marketing materials

They‚Äôre fast, familiar, and work across nearly all modern smartphones.

What Is a Redirect?

A redirect is when a web address automatically sends a visitor to another address.

For example:

  • A visitor opens sa1l.cc/abcd
  • SA1L.CC immediately forwards them to https://yourwebsite.com/page

This happens in milliseconds and feels instant to the user.

Redirects are a standard part of how the web works and are widely used for:

  • URL shortening
  • Campaign tracking
  • Site restructuring
  • Maintaining backwards compatibility

SA1L.CC uses redirects so you stay in control of where your QR codes lead.
